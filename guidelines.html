Mind Games

The objective of this game is to challenge students’ logical thinking, problem-solving skills, and teamwork.

Participants

The game is open exclusively to students from the College of Computing and Technology Engineering (CCTE).

Game Flow

Finding the Code:


The committee will provide each team with a clue that serves as a direction to find the correct code.

Teams will search for the code based on the given clue.

Teams are not allowed to remove the code from its specific location. Instead, they must take a picture of the code.

Teams must approach the committee to verify the code.

Game Flow

Verification of the Code:


If the code is correct, the team will be directed to their respective computer.

Teams will enter the code on the computer, which will reveal a logic question.

Game Flow

Solving the Logic Question:


Teams must solve the logic question displayed on the computer.

After solving the logic question, the team must approach the committee to verify their answer.

Game Flow

Next Round:


If the logic question is answered correctly, the team will receive another clue from the committee to find the next code.

The process repeats until the game concludes.

Rules and Regulations

Code Conduct:

Teams are strictly prohibited from removing the code from its designated location.

Teams found removing the code will be disqualified from the game.

If a team shares their clue with another team, it will result in disqualification and a 20-second penalty added to their recorded time.

Rules and Regulations

Verification:

Codes must be verified with the committee before proceeding to the computer.

Teams must complete the current logic question before receiving the next clue.

Rules and Regulations

Fair Play:

Teams must not interfere with other teams' progress or the placement of codes.

Collaboration between teams or sharing of answers is prohibited.

Rules and Regulations

Device Usage:

The use of mobile devices or unauthorized tools for solving clues or questions is not allowed, except for taking pictures of the codes.

Rules and Regulations

Respect and Sportsmanship:

Teams must be respectful to the committee, other participants, and the school property at all times.

Violations and Penalties

Removing a Code:

Teams found removing a code from its specific location will be disqualified.

Violations and Penalties

Sharing Clues or Answers:

Teams sharing clues or answers with other teams will be disqualified and incur a 20-second penalty to their recorded time.

Violations and Penalties

Tampering:

Tampering with other teams’ progress or clues will result in immediate disqualification.

Winning the Game

The team that completes all challenges in the shortest time wins. In case of a tie, a final logic question will decide the winner.
